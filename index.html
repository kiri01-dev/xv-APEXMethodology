Step 1 · Discovery

Discovery

The conversation · raw signal

A working conversation with customer stakeholders captures the problems and opportunities in their own words: the pain points, the constraints they're operating under, and the systems and data that exist today.

InputsStakeholder + sponsor conversation
→
OutputDiscovery Brief
What we capture

Stakeholder goals & pain

The business outcomes that matter and the vocabulary users bring to the problem.

Sponsor constraints

Scope boundaries, success criteria, and the decision authority behind the engagement.

Systems & data landscape

What systems of record exist and what data is realistically available to draw on.

Pain points & opportunities

The operational problems worth solving, often several. Shortlisting to one use case comes later, in Strategy & Refine.

Step 2 · Strategy & Refine

Strategy & Refine

Frame the terrain · agree the target

We turn the conversation into a rich Strategy Document (industry framing, data landscape, and a ranked slate of candidate use cases) plus a Domain Ontology that grounds everything downstream. We bring it back; the customer refines it and commits to a use case, now a sharper dialogue with something concrete to react to.

InputDiscovery Brief
→
OutputStrategy Document + Domain Ontology
What the Strategy Document contains

Risk / opportunity landscape

The problem situated in its domain: pressures, drivers, and where value concentrates.

Data systems inventory

The systems and feeds available to build on, mapped to the problem.

Candidate use cases

A ranked slate of buildable apps, each a potential target the customer can choose from.

Domain ontology & roadmap

Entities and relationships (the Knowledge Graph), plus a phased path from first app to scale.

Step 3 · Requirements

Requirements (PRD)

Decompose the use case into data & capabilities

We take the chosen use case and write a PRD that decomposes the app into Functional Capabilities, the reusable building blocks of what it must do. It fixes scope, data, success criteria, and governance. Because the app is a composition of known capabilities, the same pieces re-assemble across use cases: show one, and you've shown the shape of the next.

InputStrategy Doc + chosen use case
→
OutputPRD
What the PRD defines

Use Case & Users

The chosen use case, quantified as a business problem, and who reads the doc: business buyer, sign-off authority, technical buyer.

Solution & Workflow

The architecture, every named resource, and the operating workflow: what triggers a run and what happens at each stage.

Data Requirements

Real data, synthetic, or both, with schema standard, key entities, and ground-truth tagging for agent evaluation.

Success Metrics & Evaluation

The primary KPI, its quantified business value, and how both rules and agent skills are validated before sign-off.

The Functional Capabilities (the "what")
P1Ingest & ExtractTurn unstructured docs & feeds into structured records.
P2Classify & CodeApply a taxonomy: codes, labels, risk buckets.
P3Match & ReconcileCompare records across systems, surface breaks.
P4Score & DecideModel a score or a decision: approve, hold, deny.
P5Forecast & OptimizePredict future state, then optimize under constraints.
P6Monitor & AlertWatch streams for anomalies and threshold breaches.
P7Aggregate & ReportCollect, validate, and file structured outputs.
P8Orchestrate & ActAgentic multi-step execution: resolve and act.
Step 4 · Implementation Spec

Implementation Spec

Map each Capability to Platform Building Blocks

The Implementation Spec maps every Functional Capability onto concrete Platform Building Blocks: Datasource, Dataset, Model, Agent Core, Skills, Actions, Reports, and more. This is where what it must do becomes how it's built on xVector, detailed enough to build from without further Q&A.

Step 5 · App Build and Curate

App Build and Curate

A working app

FDEs and build agents assemble the app on the platform via the SDK, wiring the Building Blocks the spec named. The result is a working app on real data, evaluation pre-wired to the PRD's success criteria, human approval built in. Built from the same blocks you'd run in production, it's replicable on your data with limited change.
